02 — NAPCO / Prima
Can We Use Our Existing System?

⚠ Not Yet — Prima Is a Closed Ecosystem

NAPCO has zero automotive products. The Prima platform is locked — no API, no third-party camera integration, no RTSP stream support. We cannot promise customers their dash cam feeds into the Prima app. Any car camera we sell must run on its own app with its own subscription (or none).

✓ What Would Be Possible

Car cameras could be bundled at checkout alongside the alarm kit — same brand, same customer, separate apps. The PCAM outdoor camera already covers a parked-at-home driveway, which is a natural bridge story. If NAPCO ever opens its API, a unified backend becomes possible down the road.

06 — Key Feature
Yes, They Record When the Car Is Off

This is called Parking Mode — the camera stays awake on reserve power, watching for motion or impact. It's the single biggest selling point for security-focused buyers. Runtime: 6–18 hours on a hardwire kit; 24–40+ hours on a dedicated battery pack.

Motion-Triggered

Wakes on movement near the car. Saves a 30–60 sec clip. Most common mode. Good balance of storage and coverage.

Impact-Only

G-sensor fires on a hit or bump. Lowest battery drain. Best for long-term parking (travel, airport).

Time-Lapse

Records at 1–2 fps and compresses footage. Covers an entire day in a short clip. Good for busy urban lots.

Radar Mode ⭐

mmWave radar detects real approaching motion — no false triggers. Used by Thinkware U3000 and Nextbase iQ. Best for true security use.

08 — Effort & Risk
What It Would Actually Take

Adding a car camera line is not a small lift. This is a realistic view of what's involved before a decision is made.

What's Required

  • →3+ new supplier accounts — Thinkware, Dashcam Store Wholesale, Petra Industries. Each requires business license + resale cert.
  • →US 3PL setup — Red Stag, ShipBob, or ShipMonk. ~$250–275/mo base + per-order fees. Required since China drop-ship is no longer viable.
  • →New product content — Product pages, demo videos, install guides, comparison charts. Different audience than alarm kits.
  • →Separate app ecosystem — No Prima integration. Customers need a different app per brand (BlackVue, Thinkware, Nextbase, etc.).
  • →Customer support expansion — Hardware returns run 5–12% on electronics. Need clear process for refunds and replacements.

Risks to Consider

  • ⚠Brand split — Two product lines = two stories. Marketing focus gets divided at launch.
  • ⚠Amazon price competition — Budget and mid-range cameras are commoditized on Amazon. Margins shrink fast without a differentiated story.
  • ⚠Ring's cautionary tale — Amazon had full resources and still exited after 13 months. Positioning matters more than the product.
  • ⚠Tariff volatility — Current 40–50% China duty stack could shift again. Private-label economics are sensitive to trade policy.
  • ⚠Team bandwidth — Launching a parallel product line requires parallel execution. Worth evaluating against current capacity before committing.

09 — If We Proceed
A Possible Launch Path

If the team decides to move forward, here is a logical sequence. Timing and priority relative to the alarm product line would be determined by leadership.

Phase 1
Establish US supplier accounts Thinkware dealer (no minimum), The Dashcam Store Wholesale (multi-brand), Petra Industries (Cobra + Garmin drop-ship at $9.95). Result: 15–20 US-stocked SKUs at 20–30% margin within weeks.
Phase 2
Private-label pilot (optional, higher margin) 50–100 units from Shenzhen Zhongxiang or AZDOME. Budget: $3–10k + $268 V-Trust QC inspection + US 3PL setup. Potential: 50–70% gross margin on a branded HomeFront Watch cam.
Phase 3
Marketing — "Home Security for Your Car" Bundle battery pack with every $300+ camera (40–50% margin accessory). Target: rideshare drivers, Kia/Hyundai owners, new homeowners. The Ring Car Cam's exit in 2024 left this positioning wide open.
